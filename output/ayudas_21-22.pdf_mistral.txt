The Spanish Ministry of Education and Vocational Training announced the budget for the 2021-2022 academic year, amounting to 1550 million euros. The regulatory framework for this budget is provided by Real Decreto 471/2021, dated June 29, 2021. This budget covers various educational programs, including university studies leading to official degrees and master's titles, as well as non-university programs such as primary and secondary education, vocational training, artistic and sports education, religious studies, language courses, access and preparation courses, and basic education cycles.

For university students, scholarships cover tuition fees, which are already covered by public funding. Additionally, students receive a monthly residence allowance of 1600 euros, variable grants based on academic performance, a basic grant of 300 euros per month, and a minimum variable grant of 60 euros per month. To be eligible for these scholarships, students must have completed a minimum of 60 credits and maintain a minimum GPA of 5.0. The application period for these scholarships has not been specified in the given data.

Non-university students also have access to scholarships, but the details regarding the specific components of these scholarships were not provided in the data.
