The Spanish Ministry of Education and Vocational Training has announced the 2022-2023 academic year budget of 1,600 million euros for various education programs as outlined in Real Decreto 154/2022, dated February 22, 2022. The budget covers university programs leading to official degrees and master's titles, as well as non-university programs such as secondary education, vocational training, artistic and sports education, religious studies, language studies, access and preparation courses, and basic grade cycles.

The scholarship components include covered public fees for tuition, a fixed income of 1,700 euros, a residency grant of 1,600 euros, variable grants based on GPA, a basic grant of 300 euros, a basic grant for students in basic cycles of 350 euros, and a minimum variable grant of 60 euros.

Applicants must meet the academic requirements of a minimum of 60 credits and a GPA of 5.0 for university programs.
