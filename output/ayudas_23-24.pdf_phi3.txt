The Spanish Ministry of Education has outlined the budget for higher education in its fiscal plan covering the academic years from 2023 to 2024 with allocations totaling €1850 million under Regulation RD 117/2023 issued on February 21st. The funding is directed towards various programs within both university settings—such as degree courses leading to official titles, specialized training sessions aimed at older adult learners over age 25, master's complementary formation credits, and non-university educational offerings including secondary school subjects like arts or sports instruction, religious studies, language classes conducted by state schools, access coursework, and basic cycle formative initiatives. Scholarships provided through this scheme cover tuition fees via public funds while offering fixed income options ranging between €1700 to €2500 based on residency status; excellence grants vary according to GPA scores, starting from €50 up to €125 depending upon performance brackets. A minimum grade point average requirement stands set at 5.0. Prospective applicants can submit their applications anytime during the open window spanning March 27th until May 17th, though late submissions will be accepted till December 31st, 2023.
