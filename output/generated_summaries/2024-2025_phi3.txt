The Spanish Ministry of Education's budget for the Academic Year 2024-2025 is set at €1920 million under Regulation No. 201/2024 dated February 27th. The funding supports various educational programs including university degrees leading to official titles in both degree levels and master’s programmes; special courses aimed at older individuals seeking higher education accessibility beyond age 25 years old; supplementary training options that contribute towards obtaining a Master's title with credits awarded upon completion; as well as non-university level offerings such as primary school cycles, vocational professional formations across different tiers, advanced arts studies, sports disciplines instructional teachings, religious study pursuits on a senior scale, language learning within state schools, entry course facilitation alongside prep classes designed for entrance examination readiness into these diverse educational pathways. Scholarships provided cover tuition fees through public funds while offering fixed income support ranging from €1700 to €2500 depending on residency status along with performance bonuses based on GPA scores—€50 for GPAs between 8.00–8.49, €75 for those scoring 8.50–8.99, up to €125 for exceptional grades above 9.50.
