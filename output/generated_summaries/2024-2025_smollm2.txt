The Spanish government allocated 2536 million euros for higher education in the academic year 2024-2025 under Regulation Real Decreto 201/2024. The budget is divided between university programs and non-university programs. University programs include bachelor's degrees, master's degrees, and professional certificate courses offered by public universities. Non-university programs encompass various subjects such as arts, sports, religious studies, languages, and language proficiency courses at official education administration centers. Scholarships cover tuition fees, fixed income, residency expenses, excellence by GPA, basic grants, variable minimums, and basic cycle credits. Academic requirements mandate at least 60 credits with a minimum GPA of 0.0 for both university and non-university programs. The application period spans from March 19 to May 10, 2024, with an exception until December 31, 2024.
