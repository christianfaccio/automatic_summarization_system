The budget for the academic year 2021-2022 was 2038.13 million euros. The regulation governing this funding is Real Decreto 471/2021, published on June 29, 2021. The programs offered include university degrees leading to official bachelor's and master's degrees, preparatory courses for university entry by adults over 25 years old, and supplementary training for obtaining a master’s degree or additional credits needed for a bachelor's degree. Non-university programs cover various levels such as first and second cycle of high school education, vocational training at different levels, artistic professional studies, sports-related education, advanced artistic studies, religious higher education, language studies in officially recognized schools, preparation courses for entering vocational training, and basic vocational cycles.

Financial aid includes tuition covered by public fees, fixed income of €1700 per month, residency allowance of €1600 per month, excellence grants based on GPA ranging from €50 to €125 depending on performance, a basic grant of €300 for general purposes and €350 for those pursuing their first cycle, and a variable minimum grant that starts at €60.
