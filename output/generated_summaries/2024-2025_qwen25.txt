The budget for the academic year 2024-2025 is set at 2536 million euros. The regulation guiding this funding is Real Decreto 201/2024, issued on February 27, 2024. The programs offered include university courses leading to official degrees as well as specialized training and preparatory courses for higher education admission. Non-university programs encompass various educational pathways such as secondary education, vocational training, artistic professions, sports education, advanced arts studies, religious studies, language learning in state schools, and specific preparation courses for vocational training entrance exams conducted by public or private institutions with approved vocational training curricula.

Scholarships available consist of tuition coverage through public fees, fixed income support of 1700 euros per month, additional financial aid based on residency status (up to 2500 euros), excellence grants calculated according to GPA (ranging from 50 to 125 euros depending on performance), basic grants of 300 or 350 euros for bachelor's degree cycles, respectively, and variable minimum stipends of up to 60 euros. To be eligible for these scholarships, applicants must meet certain criteria including a minimum of 60 credits for university-level programs and a minimum GPA score.
