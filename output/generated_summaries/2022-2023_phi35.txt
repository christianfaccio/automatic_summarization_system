The Spanish Ministry of Education for the Academic Year 2022-2023 allocated a budget of €2134.29 million to fund various educational programs as per Regulation RD 154/2022 from February 22nd. The regulations set forth thresholds on family income and assets while determining scholarships' amounts based upon GPA scores—€1700 fixed tuition fee with additional grants up to €300 basic or €350 in Basic Cycle cases; variable minimum support at €60 depending on criteria met by applicants scoring between specific GPAs ranges leading to extra bonuses like €50 for those achieving 8.0–8.49 points, increasing progressively through higher score brackets ending at €125 for top scorers over 9.50. Applications were open starting March 30th until May 12th but extended till December end due to exceptional circumstances. Program offerings included university degrees requiring min 60 credits alongside non-university courses such as secondary education levels, vocational training across medium and high school stages, arts professions studies including religious institutions specializations, sports teachings, language schools under public administration control, accessibility coursework before professional certifications both within state and private sector facilities adhering strictly to defined program standards ensuring equitable student financial aid distribution
