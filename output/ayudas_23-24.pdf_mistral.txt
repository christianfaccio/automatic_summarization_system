The Spanish Ministry of Education and Vocational Training has announced the budget for the 2023-2024 academic year, which amounts to 1850 million euros. The regulatory framework for this academic year is provided by Real Decreto 117/2023, dated February 21, 2023. This budget covers various educational programs, including university studies leading to official degrees and master's titles, as well as non-university programs such as the first and second cycles of bachelor's education, professional training courses, artistic and sports education, religious studies, language studies, access and preparation courses for exams, and basic grade cycles.

For students enrolled in these programs, several scholarship components are available. Tuition fees are covered by public funds. Students also receive a fixed income of 1700 euros and a residency grant of 2500 euros per year. Additionally, there are excellence scholarships based on GPA, with varying amounts ranging from 50 to 125 euros per month depending on the student's academic performance. A basic grant of 300 euros and a higher basic grant of 350 euros for students in basic cycles are also included. Furthermore, a variable minimum grant of 60 euros per month is available for students with financial needs.
